Privacy Information Management System
Self-Assessment

Before starting the assessment, determine your organisation's role in processing Personally Identifiable Information (PII). This selection determines which controls are relevant and will be counted in your PIMS readiness evaluation.

Role selection guide: Your organisation's role may differ across PII processing activities, depending on who determines the purpose and means of processing. If your organisation determines the purpose of processing → select PII Controller. If you process on the instructions of another party → select PII Processor. If both apply to your operations → select Both.

PII Controller

The organisation that determines the purposes and means of PII processing. It has direct obligations to PII principals (data subjects) and is responsible for the lawfulness of processing.

Examples: banks, hospitals, e-commerce companies, government agencies

Table A.1 + A.3
✓
⚙️

PII Processor

The organisation that processes PII on behalf of and under the instructions of a PII Controller. It does not independently determine the purpose of processing; it works within the contractual scope defined by the controller.

Examples: cloud providers, payroll vendors, data centres, SaaS analytics

Controller & Processor

The organisation acts as a PII Controller for some activities and as a PII Processor for others. Clear separation of controls is required for each role.

Examples: technology companies that hold their own user data while also providing data processing services for other clients
